Exosphere

The exosphere is the outermost layer of Earth's atmosphere, extending from about 600 kilometers (373 miles) above the Earth's surface to around 10,000 kilometers (6,200 miles). It is where the atmosphere transitions into outer space.

This layer is characterized by extremely low densities of particles, primarily hydrogen and helium, and is where satellites orbit the Earth. The exosphere is not well-defined, as it gradually fades into the vacuum of space.

Due to its high altitude, the exosphere experiences very high temperatures, but these temperatures are not felt in the same way as at lower altitudes due to the sparse particle density.
